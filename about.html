My Masters Research Project

My undergraduate dissertation was about the belief in free will, depression and subjective well-being. You can read about it here.

Although I learned a lot from the process, I was not completely happy with my methods on the project.

For one reason, research into the belief in free will has not done enough to show that, when measuring the belief, you are not simply measuring a similar, more established construct like Locus of Control or Self-Efficacy.

It turns out that there are new scales designed to measure new contructs of perceived control being designed quite often. And I have not seen any that sufficiently examine whether or not the measure actually taps into a new construct, or just part of an old construct.

I am trying to address this issue through my Masters research project, where I am testing the discriminant validity of scales related to perceived control and examining the possibility of a general factor of perceived control.

Although the latent nature of psychological constructs mean you cannot address these issues with certainty, you can see how appropriate/useful various measurement models might be.

I intend to do this by collecting data from various scales purporting to measure unique constructs and performing various SEM-like techniques on the data.

I hope to create an accessible report of my methods and results, that will probably be hosted on this site.
